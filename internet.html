Internet Safety


The Internet is kind of like a giant city of people.

Like any city, there are lots of great places to visit and people to meet.

However, there are also places we need to avoid and people we should not talk to.

We need all the ninjas to think about how they use the Internet and follow some simple rules on what we do.

These rules are for everyone to help make our Dojo better. If you think a new rule or a change to an existing rule that could help everyone, we can all decide if we should make the rule change.

Rule 1: Be Kind

One of the ways to make the Internet better, is to be kind to everyone online.

Sadly there are some people on the Internet that will say and share hurtful things, use bad language and lie. If this happens, never ever respond to them and ask a mentor or adult for advice on what to do.

Cyberbullying is when people are nasty to other people online. We hope none of our Ninjas ever bully anyone and consider it a very serious breaking of our rules and code of conduct.

Ninjas should never worry about reporting cyberbullying or anything they do not like to a mentor or adult at CoderDojo.


Rule 2: Protect and Respect Privacy

Privacy is a fundamental right protecting individuals' rights to control their personal information and prevent unauthorized intrusion into their private lives. This means, people have the right to choose how information on them is used.

  • We need to learn how to protect our privacy from companies and people on the Internet looking to exploit (use for a bad reason) our personal data.
  • We need to understand how to control what gets shared and how to keep our data and applications safe.
  • We need to respect the privacy of others and not share their personal information online.
  • We have to think about what we say or photos we share when online especially if other people are involved.

Privacy is a very complex and often confusing concept. Technology advances very quickly, however laws needs to be considered and thought about for a long time before governments make big changes to how we use technology. It can take many years after a problem is found before laws are created to try and fix it.

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) was designed to address how companies store data on people. It provides everyone the right to know what information a company holds on them along with rights on processing and retention (how long companies can keep data).

GDPR regulations also required EU member states to establish an Age of Digital Consent. The digital age of consent is the minimum age a user must be before a social media or internet company can collect, process and store their data. In Ireland, the Age of Digital Consent is sixteen.

  • Ninjas should protect their privacy and what they share from.
  • Ninjas should never disclose or publicise personal information.

Rule 3: Don't Talk to Strangers

The Internet is designed for communicating and sharing information with people and is super important to the world we live.

Unfortunately their are also cybercriminals trying to trick people to share more personal details then they should.

Phishing is a form of social engineering and a scam where attackers deceive people into revealing sensitive information or installing malware such as viruses, worms, adware, or ransomware.

Anyone can pretend to be someone else online and it can be hard to know if someone is who they say they are. Even if someone looks, sounds, or acts like someone your age, they may not be.

  • Never respond to a message from someone you don't know.
  • Try not to open messages unless you know who they are from.
  • If anyone ever asks you for something and you are unsure, ask an adult or mentor for advice.

Rule 4: Be Media Smart

There are many different ways that information is shared for example television, radio broadcasts, articles in newspapers, advertisements, games, comic books, memes and many more. These methods of communication are known as media.

When we read an article in a newspaper like the Letirim Oberserver or watch the RT√â News, we know the information will be written by a journalist or reporter that has learn how to present their stories with facts and evidence to support them and will be reviewed by an editor to ensure there are no mistakes or incorrect information.

One of the great parts of the Internet is that anyone can create a webpage and start writing stories, or create a Youtube channel or TikTok videos on any subject they like. This also creates a big challenge about what and who we should trust online. It can be difficult to judge how accurate or reliable information is; and we should always Stop, Think and Check when we see new information online or before we send on media to someone else.

Stop

  • Read more than the headline
  • Don't assume the picture is giving you the full story
  • Just because information goes viral, doesn't mean it is true or accurate

Think

  • Ask yourself what the purpose of the information is
  • Consider your own biases
  • See if the information is being reported anywhere else

Check

  • Check the source
  • Look closely at the web address
  • Look at the detail to check for accuracy
  • Ask an expert
